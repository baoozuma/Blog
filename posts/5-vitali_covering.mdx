## Setup

Let $(X, d)$ be a separable metric space and $\mathcal{F}$ a collection of closed balls.
Define

$$
R = \sup\{r \mid B(x,r) \in \mathcal{F}\}
$$

and partition $\mathcal{F}$ into layers by radius:

$$
F_i = \{B(x,r) \in \mathcal{F} \mid R \cdot 2^{-i-1} \leq r \leq R \cdot 2^{-i}\}
$$

## Constructing the Disjoint Subcollection

Define a sequence of maximal disjoint subcollections $(G_n)$ inductively.

**Base case.** Let $H_0 = F_0$ and define the poset

$$
P = \{F \subseteq H_0 \mid F \text{ pairwise disjoint}\}, \qquad F_1 \leq F_2 \iff F_1 \subseteq F_2
$$

**Zorn's condition.** Given a chain $F_1 \leq F_2 \leq \cdots$, let $F = \bigcup_{n=1}^\infty F_n$.
For any $B_1, B_2 \in F$, there exists $k$ large enough so that $B_1, B_2 \in F_k$,
hence $B_1 \cap B_2 = \varnothing$. Thus every chain has an upper bound in $P$,
so by Zorn's lemma $P$ has a maximal element $G_0$.

**Inductive step.** Given $G_1, \ldots, G_n$, set

$$
H_{n+1} = \{B \in F_{n+1} \mid B \cap C = \varnothing,\ \forall C \in G_1 \cup \cdots \cup G_n\}
$$

Apply Zorn's lemma again to obtain a maximal disjoint subcollection $G_{n+1} \subseteq H_{n+1}$.

## Countability of $G$

**Claim.** $G = \bigcup_{n=1}^\infty G_n$ is countable.

Since $X$ is separable, it admits a countable cover by closed balls
$\{B(y_i, R \cdot 2^{i-1})\}_{i \in \mathbb{N}}$.
We claim each such ball contains **at most one center** of a ball in $G_i$.

Suppose $B(x_1, r_1), B(x_2, r_2) \in G_i$ with $x_1, x_2 \in B(y, R \cdot 2^{i-2})$ for some $y$. Then

$$
\|x_1 - x_2\| \leq \|x_1 - y\| + \|y - x_2\| \leq R \cdot 2^{i-1} \leq r_1, r_2
$$

so $x_1 \in B(x_2, r_2)$ and $x_2 \in B(x_1, r_1)$, contradicting disjointness of $G_i$.
Hence each $G_i$ is countable, and so is $G$. $\square$

## The 5B Covering

**Claim.** $\displaystyle\bigcup_{B \in \mathcal{F}} B \subseteq 5G := \bigcup_{B \in G} 5B$.

Let $B(x_1, r_1) \in \mathcal{F}$. Then $B(x_1,r_1) \in F_i$ for some $i$, so

$$
R \cdot 2^{-i-1} < r_1 \leq R \cdot 2^{-i}
$$

If $B(x_1, r_1) \notin G_i$, by maximality of $G_i$ there exists $B(x_2, r_2) \in G_i$
with $B(x_1, r_1) \cap B(x_2, r_2) \neq \varnothing$.

For any $x \in B(x_1, r_1)$:

$$
\|x_2 - x\| \leq \|x_1 - x\| + \|x_2 - x_1\| \leq r_1 + r_1 + r_2 = 2r_1 + r_2
$$

To find the best constant $k$ such that $2r_1 + r_2 \leq k r_2$, note that both
$r_1, r_2 \in (R \cdot 2^{-i-1}, R \cdot 2^{-i}]$, so

$$
\frac{2r_1}{r_2} + 1 \leq \frac{2 \cdot R \cdot 2^{-i}}{R \cdot 2^{-i-1}} + 1 = 4 + 1 = 5
$$

Therefore

$$
\|x_2 - x\| \leq 5r_2
$$

which gives $B(x_1, r_1) \subseteq 5B(x_2, r_2)$. Since $B(x_1,r_1) \in \mathcal{F}$ was arbitrary,

$$
\bigcup_{B \in \mathcal{F}} B \subseteq \bigcup_{B \in G} 5B = 5G \qquad \blacksquare
$$
